About

Hi, I'm Azad 👋

I'm a software engineer who cares a lot about building things that actually matter. I like working close to the product: understanding what's really going on, spotting bottlenecks, and fixing the parts that slow people down (both in code and in process).

Teams usually see me as the one they can rely on when things get messy. The kind of problems where nothing is clearly broken, but something isn't right. That's where I do my best work.

I think a lot about software architecture to design systems that don't fall apart under real-world pressure. One project I'm especially proud of is Araamma, an AI-powered therapy and mental health platform. It wasn't just about shipping features … it was about building something that could genuinely help people.

For me, good software is an artistic piece of text that makes the computer work properly … but also something that other humans can read without losing their sanity.

Outside of work, I'm usually playing guitar, singing, running a D&D session, gaming, or cooking something new. I like things that require a bit of creativity and a bit of structure … same reason I enjoy programming, I guess.

If that sounds like your kind of environment, we'll probably get along 🙂
